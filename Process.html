Good to know

Questions about the process

How long does therapy take?

It depends on the modality and what you are working through. EMDR for a single incident often resolves in 3-8 sessions. Complex trauma takes longer. Ketamine-assisted therapy typically produces noticeable change within hours of the first infusion, followed by integration sessions. For most clients, meaningful change is visible within 8-12 sessions. I give you a realistic range in the free consultation based on your specific history.

I have tried therapy before and it did not help. Why would this be different?

Most therapy that does not work is a modality mismatch, not a personal failure. If anxiety or depression is rooted in unprocessed trauma, traditional talk therapy often plateaus. EMDR, IFS, and ketamine-assisted therapy work through fundamentally different mechanisms. I build the plan after learning your history and what you have already tried, so we are not repeating an approach that did not land.

Do I have to describe traumatic events in detail?

No. EMDR and IFS work without requiring you to narrate your history aloud. You hold the content; we work with the structure underneath it. Some clients find it meaningful to talk through what happened. Others do not. Both paths reach the same outcome.

What if I want to change the approach mid-treatment?

That is not a problem. The plan evolves based on how you respond. If you want to shift focus or try a different modality, we talk about it and adjust. You are not locked into a protocol.

How much does it cost, and do you accept insurance?

My practice is out-of-network. Rates are discussed openly in the free consultation. Many clients with PPO plans receive significant reimbursement using a superbill I provide. I respond to all cost questions directly and honestly before you commit to anything.
