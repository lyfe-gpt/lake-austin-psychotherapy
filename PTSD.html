PTSD treatment questions

What to expect

What is the most effective treatment for PTSD?

EMDR (Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing) is the gold-standard evidence-based treatment for PTSD, recommended by the World Health Organization, the American Psychological Association, and the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs. Unlike talk therapy, EMDR does not require detailed retelling of traumatic events. It works by reprocessing the memory itself so that it no longer triggers the same emotional and physical response.

How does EMDR work for trauma?

EMDR uses bilateral stimulation (typically eye movements) while you briefly focus on a distressing memory. This appears to allow the brain to reprocess the memory the way it processes ordinary experiences during REM sleep, removing the stuck emotional charge. Most clients find they can recall the event without the same intensity of distress after processing. Sessions are available remotely throughout Texas.

Do I have to describe the traumatic event in detail?

No. One of the key benefits of EMDR is that detailed verbal retelling is not required. You hold the memory in mind briefly during processing, but you do not need to narrate it. This makes EMDR accessible for people who have found traditional talk therapy re-traumatizing or difficult.

How many EMDR sessions does PTSD treatment take?

For a single-incident trauma, EMDR can produce significant relief in 3 to 8 sessions. Complex or developmental trauma, or PTSD with multiple events, typically takes longer. The free consultation gives you a realistic picture of the timeline based on your specific history.

Can PTSD treatment be done via telehealth?

Yes. EMDR and IFS therapy for PTSD are both available via secure, HIPAA-compliant video anywhere in Texas. Research supports telehealth EMDR as equally effective as in-person for trauma. You need to be physically located in Texas during sessions.
