Depression treatment questions

What to expect

Which therapy works best for depression?

The right approach depends on what is driving the depression. When depression is rooted in past trauma or unprocessed experiences, EMDR often produces faster results than traditional talk therapy. CBT helps change the thought patterns that maintain depression day to day. Ketamine-assisted psychotherapy is available for treatment-resistant depression that has not responded to medication or other therapy. Your free consultation identifies which path fits your history and goals.

Is ketamine therapy effective for treatment-resistant depression?

Yes. Ketamine-assisted psychotherapy is one of the most well-supported interventions for treatment-resistant depression. Multiple clinical trials show rapid improvement, often within hours of the first infusion. The physician-administered protocol pairs infusions with ongoing integration therapy to convert the neuroplastic window ketamine opens into lasting change.

Can I do depression treatment via telehealth?

Yes. EMDR, CBT, and talk therapy for depression are all available via secure, HIPAA-compliant video anywhere in Texas. Research consistently shows telehealth therapy achieves outcomes equivalent to in-person care for depression. Ketamine infusions are in-person in Austin; all other therapy is available statewide.

How long does depression treatment take?

For depression with a clear traumatic root, EMDR can produce significant relief in weeks. Ketamine-assisted therapy often brings rapid mood improvement that integration therapy then stabilizes over time. Chronic or complex depression typically requires longer-term work. Your free consultation gives you a realistic picture of timeline based on your specific history.

Do you treat anxiety and PTSD as well?

Yes. I treat depression, anxiety, PTSD, trauma, prolonged grief, and alcohol use disorder. Many clients present with overlapping conditions, and my approach builds a plan that addresses the full picture, not just a single diagnosis.
