When to seek support

Signs your grief may need more than time

Grief that hasn’t softened after a year or more
Difficulty sleeping or functioning at work
Intense longing or yearning that hasn’t eased
Withdrawing from relationships or activities
Feeling that life has lost meaning
Unable to access positive memories of the person

If grief is still significantly affecting your daily life, support is available regardless of when the loss occurred.

How I work

More than supportive listening.

Grief therapy is structured and intentional. The goal is not to process grief faster, but to address what is keeping it frozen so you can move through it.

01

Understanding your loss

We start by understanding the nature of the loss, how it is showing up for you, what has helped and what hasn’t, and what you most want to feel differently about.

02

Working with what is stuck

Using EMDR and IFS, we work with the patterns and parts of you that are keeping grief frozen. This is not about retelling the story. It is about addressing the structure underneath it.

03

Rebuilding forward

As grief softens, we work on what life looks like going forward, identity, relationships, meaning. Grief does not disappear. The goal is to carry it without being consumed by it.

Questions

Good to know

How do I know if grief therapy is right for me?

If grief has not softened with time, is affecting your sleep, work, or relationships, or feels like it has changed who you are, therapy can help. You do not need a diagnosis. A short discovery call is enough to figure out whether it’s the right fit.

What approaches do you use for grief?

I use EMDR and IFS to work with grief that has become stuck. EMDR addresses the way loss is stored in the nervous system. IFS helps with the protective parts that are keeping grief frozen. Both are structured and evidence-based, not just supportive listening.

Do I have to talk about the loss in detail?

No. Grief therapy is not about retelling the story of the loss repeatedly. The work focuses on what is keeping grief frozen, which may involve the loss but does not require you to narrate it in detail. The pace is yours to set.

Is grief therapy available via telehealth?

Yes. Grief therapy is available via secure video throughout Texas. Both EMDR and IFS translate well to telehealth and do not require in-person attendance.
