How it works

The brain already knows how to heal. EMDR gets out of its way.

01

Assessment & preparation

We map the memories and triggers to address, build stabilization resources, and ensure you have the tools to stay regulated between sessions.

02

Bilateral stimulation sets

You hold a targeted memory in mind while following guided eye movements, taps, or tones. The bilateral stimulation activates the same process the brain uses during REM sleep to consolidate and neutralize distressing material.

03

Integration

As memories reprocess, their emotional charge diminishes and new, adaptive beliefs take hold. We close each session carefully and continue building on progress in subsequent work.

What it helps with

Not just PTSD. EMDR reaches further than most people realize.

EMDR was originally developed for PTSD, where it has the strongest evidence. The research has since extended to anxiety, depression, grief, phobias, and performance concerns.

Questions

Good to know

What is EMDR therapy?

EMDR (Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing) is an evidence-based therapy that uses bilateral stimulation to help the brain reprocess traumatic memories. Unlike talk therapy, it does not require detailed verbal narration of what happened. The WHO and American Psychological Association both recognize it as a first-line treatment for PTSD.

Do I have to relive my trauma during EMDR?

No. You hold a memory in mind while following bilateral stimulation. You do not have to describe it in detail or talk through what happened. Processing occurs internally, which is why many people find EMDR easier to tolerate than traditional trauma-focused talk therapy.

How many sessions does EMDR take?

Single-incident trauma often resolves in 6 to 12 sessions. Complex or developmental trauma requires more. Most clients notice meaningful change within the first few processing sessions. We discuss realistic expectations during your free consultation.

Is EMDR available via telehealth?

Yes. EMDR is fully available via secure video throughout Texas. Online bilateral stimulation tools work effectively, and the research on telehealth EMDR shows outcomes comparable to in-person.
