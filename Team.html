Training & modalities

What I use in the work

EMDR Therapy

Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing. The gold-standard, evidence-based treatment for PTSD, recommended by the WHO, APA, and VA. I completed EMDRIA-approved basic training and continue supervision hours.

Internal Family Systems (IFS)

A parts-based approach that helps clients understand and heal the different "parts" of themselves, including protective and exiled parts rooted in past experience. Non-pathologizing and deeply effective for trauma and self-criticism.

Ketamine-Assisted Psychotherapy

KAP integration therapy paired with physician-supervised ketamine sessions. I provide preparation and integration support: the therapeutic work that converts the neuroplastic window ketamine opens into lasting change.

Prolonged Grief Disorder Protocol

Advanced training in the Columbia University PGD protocol, the most evidence-based treatment for grief that has lasted 12+ months and disrupted daily life. 70% efficacy rate in clinical trials.

About working with me

Questions you might have

Who will I be working with?

You’ll work directly with me, Courtney Kurtz, LPC-A. I don’t hand you off to another counselor. Every session is with me, from first contact through ongoing care.

What is an LPC-A and how does supervision work?

LPC-A stands for Licensed Professional Counselor Associate: the Texas designation for counselors who hold a graduate degree, have passed the NCE national licensure exam, and are completing supervised clinical hours toward full LPC licensure. I work under the supervision of a licensed clinical supervisor, which is a standard and common part of counselor development in Texas. Your care is fully credentialed and ethically supervised.

What is EMDR training and why does it matter?

EMDR training involves completing an EMDRIA-approved basic training, followed by supervised consultation hours. I am EMDR-trained and continue consultation to deepen this work. EMDR is the gold-standard, evidence-based treatment for PTSD, recommended by the WHO, APA, and VA. Look for an EMDR-trained therapist with ongoing supervision, not just a weekend workshop.

Where was Courtney Kurtz trained?

I completed my graduate training in counseling and have pursued advanced training in EMDR through an EMDRIA-approved program, Internal Family Systems (IFS), and ketamine-assisted psychotherapy (KAP) integration. I also completed training in the Columbia University Prolonged Grief Disorder protocol. I receive ongoing clinical supervision and professional development.
