About the practice

Questions about our team

Who will I be working with?

Your free consultation matches you with the right clinician for your goals. Mary K. Moore, LPC, is the clinical director and specializes in EMDR, prolonged grief, depression, and trauma. Courtney Kurtz, Sally Reyes, Hannah Alexander, Josh Shifrin, and Arlee Pryor cover anxiety, couples therapy, adolescents, women’s issues, and alcohol use disorder. Dr. Truman Milling Jr., MD oversees all ketamine-assisted sessions.

What is EMDRIA certification and why does it matter?

EMDRIA certification requires completing 50+ EMDR sessions with 25+ clients, 20+ hours of consultation with an approved consultant, and ongoing continuing education. It is the highest credential in EMDR practice. Most providers are only “EMDR-trained” (a basic weekend course). Mary K. Moore holds full EMDRIA certification, meaning her clinical approach is rigorously validated.

Is a physician present during ketamine sessions?

Yes. Dr. Truman Milling Jr., MD, FACEP is present bedside for the complete infusion. He is a board-certified emergency physician with 60+ peer-reviewed publications funded by the NHLBI/NIH. Most ketamine clinics do not have a physician present throughout the session. This is one of the practice’s highest safety standards.

Where was Mary K. Moore trained?

Mary K. Moore trained in CBT at Harvard Medical School’s Psychiatric Academy (MGH), and trained directly with Columbia University’s Center for Complicated Grief, the team that developed the clinical protocol for Prolonged Grief Disorder. She holds full EMDRIA certification and is a member of the ACA task force, EMDRIA, and the Somatic Experiencing Trauma Institute.
